# Narrative Designer

## UMBRAL - Narrative Framework

**Story Premise**
A child's shadow detaches during a moment of profound fear, watching helplessly as its owner is taken away by strangers. The shadow must journey through a world where memories live in darkness, manipulating light to reveal the path home. But home may not be what it remembers, and reunion may require becoming something new.

**Protagonist (Through Action)**
The shadow reaches toward a flickering candle, then pulls back as light sears its edges. It stretches itself thin across three walls to bridge a gap, trembling with effort. When it finds a memory-fragment of its child drawing in dirt, it traces the same shapes with its own form, practicing. At a broken mirror, it splits itself deliberately, testing whether it can still be whole after division. When light threatens to erase it completely, it wraps around a fallen bird, sheltering it from the harsh noon sun.

**World (Sensory Detail)**
Paper whispers under footsteps that cast no prints. Ink pools where shadows have lingered too long, staining white walls with memory. Lanterns breathe like sleeping lungs, their light expanding and contracting with an unnamed heartbeat. Wind through windows carries the scent of wet brushes and burnt matches. Shadows move wrong here—stretching toward darkness instead of light, growing solid enough to bear weight, leaving traces of warmth on cold stone.

**Central Conflict**
*External:* Navigate a labyrinth of light and shadow to reach the place where child and shadow were separated, manipulating an environment that grows increasingly hostile to its existence.
*Internal:* Confront the truth that reunification might mean erasure—that to truly help its child, the shadow may need to let go of what it was and become what is needed.

## Three-Act Structure

**Act I: Separation**
The shadow awakens alone in a white room. Learns basic movement and light manipulation. Discovers first memory-fragment: child hiding under bed, afraid of footsteps in the hallway. Shadow gains ability to stretch.

**Act II: Journey**
The shadow navigates increasingly complex light puzzles, each tied to a memory of growing fear and isolation. Learns to split, reform, and manipulate multiple light sources. The memories reveal why the child was taken: illness that makes light painful, darkness a refuge. Shadow begins to understand that light isn't enemy—it's medicine the child needed but feared.

**Act III: Recognition**
The shadow reaches a hospital room where its child lies sleeping. Realizes the "strangers" were doctors, the separation was surgery under bright lights. Must choose: reunite as the fearful shadow it was, or become something that can dance with light instead of fleeing from it.

## Three Key Dialogue Scenes

**Scene 1 - Memory Fragment: The Last Night**
*[Shadow touches a dark stain on a child's bedroom wall. A figure materializes—translucent, young, hugging its knees]*

**CHILD:** Why do the lights hurt now?

**MOTHER'S VOICE (from hallway):** The doctors can help. But you have to let them.

**CHILD:** What if... what if I'm not me anymore after?

**MOTHER'S VOICE:** You'll always be you. Even the parts you can't see.

*[Child looks down, notices shadow is wrong—detached, reaching toward the window]*

**CHILD:** Are you scared too?

*[Memory dissolves. Shadow is alone, understanding: they were both afraid of change]*

---

**Scene 2 - The Doctor's Garden**
*[Shadow manipulates sunlight through garden mirrors, creating a path. Discovers a memory-fragment of the child in a wheelchair, a doctor kneeling beside them]*

**DOCTOR:** Look.

*[She adjusts a prism. Rainbow light dances on the child's hands]*

**DOCTOR:** Light isn't something that happens to you. It's something you can... conduct.

**CHILD:** Like music?

**DOCTOR:** Like breathing. In, out. Rest, movement.

**CHILD:** Will my shadow come back?

**DOCTOR:** Shadows change. Sometimes they have to leave to learn how to return.

*[Child moves hand. Rainbow shifts. Child almost smiles]*

**CHILD:** It's... not hurting.

---

**Scene 3 - The Choice**
*[Hospital room at golden hour. Child sleeps in filtered light. Shadow hovers at threshold, wavering]*

**CHILD (eyes still closed):** I know you're there.

*[Long pause. Shadow moves closer but doesn't touch]*

**CHILD:** I'm different now. The light doesn't... we don't have to hide anymore.

*[Child opens eyes, looks toward window where shadow wavers between two light sources]*

**CHILD:** You can stay scared. Or...

*[Child reaches toward window. Shadow must choose: flee to the dark corner, or meet the reaching hand in the light]*

**CHILD:** We can learn together.

*[Shadow stretches across sunbeam, trembling but holding steady. For first time, light doesn't burn—it reveals]*

## The Ending

The shadow chooses to merge not in darkness but in gentle light. As they reunite, both are transformed: the child no longer fears illumination, the shadow no longer flees from it. They cast new shapes together—not the cowering forms of before, but dancing silhouettes that play between light and dark. 

The final image: child's hand moving in a sunbeam, shadow-puppet animals leaping across the hospital wall. Other children gather to watch. The shadow has learned that connection doesn't require hiding—sometimes it requires being seen.

The last sound: paper settling. A new story ready to be written.

*[Fade to white paper, single black brushstroke, then—silence]*
